## 8. Suggested Database & App Changes Based on Day Book Analysis

Based on this robust daybook analysis, we suggest the following optimizations and changes for your analytical setup:

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Voucher Type Segmentation:** The Flutter frontend should support filtering and viewing reports by specific sales types. Currently, 'Head Office Sales' makes up over 72% of your revenue, Pasal is retail store sales, and Bafal Sales is another branch. Separating these channels in the UI will give much better operational insights.

> [!TIP]
> **Purchasing Analysis Integration:** Since the Day Book contains both sales and purchases, we can link them to track product profit margins directly in the database! By mapping each product sold to its latest purchase rate (which covers 92.3% of sold items), the backend can dynamically estimate real-time cost and profit margins, rather than relying on manual exports.

> [!WARNING]
> ** Nepal-Specific Calendar Tracking:** Sales on Saturdays (the official weekend in Nepal) are very low, while Sundays show massive spikes. This indicates that your dispatch operations are highly concentrated on Sundays. Setting up automated inventory replenishment alerts on slow days (Tuesdays/Saturdays) can help optimize the sunday rush.

> [!NOTE]
> **Accounts Receivable/Payable tracking:** Since the Day Book records Payments (NPR 310M+) and Receipts (NPR 289M+), we can expand the backend API to track customer ledger balances and outstanding payments, letting the Flutter app show debtor/creditor aging directly from Tally.
